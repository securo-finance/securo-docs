Accounts in Securo represent your real-world financial accounts: bank accounts, wallets, credit cards, or any container that holds money. Every transaction belongs to an account, and your total balance is calculated by summing all account balances.

![Accounts](/screenshots/accounts.png)

## Account Types

Securo organizes accounts into two categories:

### Manual Accounts

These are accounts you manage by hand. You create them, set a starting balance, and manually add transactions (or import them from CSV/OFX files). This includes:

- **Checking accounts**
- **Savings accounts**
- **Cash wallets**
- **Credit cards**

### Bank Connections

If you connect your bank through the bank sync feature, those accounts appear in a separate section. Connected accounts can automatically import transactions from your bank.

## Multi-Currency Support

One of Securo's standout features is **native multi-currency support**. Each account has its own currency, and Securo handles conversion automatically.

For example, you might have:
- A checking account in **BRL** (Brazilian Real)
- A savings account in **BRL**
- An investment account in **USD** (US Dollar)

The dashboard and reports will show each account's balance in its native currency, plus the converted equivalent in your primary currency. This makes it easy to see your total net worth across currencies.

## Creating an Account

1. Navigate to **Accounts** in the sidebar
2. Click **+ Add Account**
3. Enter the account name
4. Select the account type (Checking, Savings, Credit Card, etc.)
5. Choose the currency
6. Set the initial balance
7. Click **Save**

The initial balance represents how much is in the account when you start tracking it in Securo. All future transactions will be calculated relative to this starting point.

## Viewing Account Details

Click on any account in the sidebar or the accounts page to see:

- Current balance with percentage change
- Account type and currency
- A filtered list of all transactions for that account

## Account Sidebar

Your accounts are always visible in the main sidebar, showing the account name, type, current balance, and a percentage change indicator. This gives you a quick glance at all your balances without navigating away from whatever page you're on.
