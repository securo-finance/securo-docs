---
title: Groups
description: Split shared expenses with people, projects, or cost centers and track who owes whom.
---

Groups let you split shared expenses — a dinner with friends, a trip, a shared apartment, or a project budget — and keep track of who owes whom. When you split a transaction through a group, Securo records each person's share and calculates balances automatically.

![Groups](/screenshots/groups.png)

## Creating a Group

Go to **Groups** in the sidebar and click **+ Add group**. Fill in:

1. **Name**: What the group is for (e.g., "Apartment", "Lisbon Trip")
2. **Type**: The kind of group, which drives labels and reports
3. **Default currency**: The currency used for balances
4. **Notes** (optional): Any extra context

Groups can be one of these types:

| Type | Use for |
|------|---------|
| **Social** | Friends, family, roommates |
| **Cost center** | Internal departments or teams |
| **Project** | A specific project or initiative |
| **Client** | Client-related expenses |
| **Other** | Anything else |

## Members

A group's **members** are the people who share its expenses. Open a group and click **Add member** to add one. You can either:

- **Link a Securo user** — select an existing user from the directory. Their name and email fill in automatically, and the group is shared with them.
- **Add a non-user** — just enter a name, and optionally an email. Useful for people who don't have a Securo account.

## Splitting a Transaction

To split an expense, open the **Add** or **Edit** transaction dialog and check **Split this transaction**. Then:

1. Choose the **group**
2. Choose how to **split by**:
   - **Equal** — the amount is divided evenly among the selected members
   - **Exact amounts** — you enter each member's exact share
   - **Percentages** — you enter each member's percentage
3. Select which members are part of the split

For exact amounts and percentages, Securo shows a running total so you can confirm the shares add up before saving.

### Adding Multiple Transactions at Once

To split many transactions the same way, select them on the [Transactions](/docs/features/transactions) page and choose **Add to group**. Pick the group and a split rule — **Equal** or **Percentages** — to apply to all of them. Transfers and already-split transactions are skipped automatically.

## The Group Page

Opening a group shows everything in one place:

- **Total moved**, **Owed to you**, and **You owe** summary cards
- **Spending trend** and **Spending by category** charts
- The **Members** list
- **Balances** — who owes whom, computed from splits minus settlements
- **Recent transactions** split through the group
- **Settlements** that have been recorded

## Settling Up

When someone pays back their share, record it as a **settlement**. Click **Settle up** on a balance line — or **Record settlement** — and confirm who paid whom, the amount, the date, and optional notes. The balances update immediately.

If you're the one paying, you can tick **Also record as a transaction in my account** to create a matching transaction in one of your accounts, linked to the settlement.

## Shared Transactions

When you split a transaction, members who are linked Securo users see it in their own transaction list — but only their **share**, marked as **Shared** and labeled with who paid. These rows are read-only and don't count toward the member's personal income or expenses, only toward the group balances.

## Archiving Groups

When a group is no longer active, edit it and tick **Archived**. Archived groups are hidden from the default view — use the **Active**, **Archived**, and **All** filters at the top of the Groups page to switch between them. You can un-archive a group at any time, or delete it entirely.
