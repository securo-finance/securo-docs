Bank Sync lets you connect your real bank accounts to Securo so transactions are imported and updated automatically. Instead of manually entering every expense, your bank data flows into Securo on a recurring schedule, gets categorized, and stays in sync.

Securo's provider system is designed to support multiple bank sync integrations. Each provider covers a different region or set of financial institutions.

## Supported Providers

| Provider | Region | Status |
|----------|--------|--------|
| [Pluggy](https://pluggy.ai) | Brazil | Available |
| EU provider | Europe | Coming soon |
| US provider | United States | Coming soon |

We are actively working on expanding coverage to European and US banks. For now, Pluggy covers Brazilian financial institutions.

## How Bank Sync Works

1. You set up credentials with a supported provider
2. You configure those credentials in Securo
3. On the **Accounts** page, click **Connect Bank** to link your bank
4. Securo imports your accounts and full transaction history
5. A background task keeps everything in sync automatically

## Pluggy (Brazilian Banks)

[Pluggy](https://pluggy.ai) is a Brazilian open finance platform that connects to major banks and financial institutions in Brazil. Securo embeds the Pluggy Connect Widget directly in the app, so the entire bank authentication flow happens without leaving Securo.

### 1. Create a Pluggy Account

1. Go to [dashboard.pluggy.ai](https://dashboard.pluggy.ai) and create an account
2. Create a new **Team** in the dashboard
3. Create a new **Application** under your team
4. Copy the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** from your application

![Pluggy Credentials](/screenshots/pluggy-dashboard-credentials.png)

You can browse the **Connectors** section to see which banks and institutions are supported:

![Pluggy Connectors](/screenshots/pluggy-dashboard-connectors.png)

<Callout type="warn">
Pluggy offers a free trial with limited connections. Make sure your trial is active before attempting to connect bank accounts in Securo.
</Callout>

### 2. Test Your Credentials with MeuPluggy (Optional)

[MeuPluggy](https://meu.pluggy.ai) is a free portal provided by Pluggy that lets you test your API credentials and verify that your bank connection works before configuring Securo.

1. Go to [meu.pluggy.ai](https://meu.pluggy.ai) and sign in
2. Enter the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** from your application
3. Try connecting a bank account to confirm your credentials are valid

![MeuPluggy](/screenshots/pluggy-meu-intro.png)

This step is optional but recommended if you want to verify everything works on Pluggy's side before setting up Securo.

### 3. Configure Securo

Add your Pluggy credentials to the Securo `.env` file:

```bash
PLUGGY_CLIENT_ID=your-client-id-here
PLUGGY_CLIENT_SECRET=your-client-secret-here
```

Restart the backend after updating the environment. Securo automatically detects configured providers on startup and makes Pluggy available as a connection option.

### 4. Connect Your Bank

1. Go to the **Accounts** page and click **Connect Bank**
2. A dialog shows all available providers — select **Pluggy**
3. Securo requests a connect token from the backend and opens the Pluggy Connect Widget directly inside the app
4. Select for the MeuPluggy 
5. Choose your bank account that you already connected on [MeuPluggy](https://meu.pluggy.ai)
6. Complete any MFA challenges if prompted by your bank
7. Once authenticated, the widget returns the connection to Securo automatically
8. Securo imports your accounts and full transaction history immediately

![Connect Bank](/screenshots/pluggy-connect-bank.png)

You don't need to copy any IDs or leave the app. The widget handles the entire authentication flow, and Securo stores the connection internally. After connecting, your bank accounts appear alongside manual accounts on the Accounts page.

### Reconnecting

If a connection expires or encounters an error, click **Reconnect** on the affected connection. Securo reopens the widget using your existing connection, so you just need to re-authenticate with your bank. No new connection is created — the existing one is updated.

## Sync Behavior

### Automatic Sync

A background task runs every hour and refreshes any connection that hasn't been synced in the last 4 hours. Your transactions stay up to date without manual intervention.

### Manual Sync

You can trigger an immediate sync from the Accounts page by clicking the sync icon next to a bank connection.

### What Happens During Sync

- Account balances are updated
- New transactions since the last sync are imported
- Pending transactions are included or excluded based on your settings
- Duplicate transactions (already imported) are skipped
- Manually entered transactions are matched against synced ones using fuzzy matching (same amount, similar date, similar description)
- Categories are auto-mapped from the provider's categories to your Securo categories
- Transfer pairs between accounts are detected automatically

## Connection Settings

Each bank connection has settings that control how transactions are imported.

### Payee Source

Controls how the payee name is extracted from bank data:

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Auto** (default) | Tries merchant name first, then payment data, then falls back to none |
| **Merchant** | Uses the merchant name from the transaction |
| **Payment Data** | Uses payer/receiver information from payment details |
| **Description** | Uses the raw bank transaction description |
| **None** | Does not extract a payee |

### Import Pending

When enabled (default), pending transactions are imported alongside posted ones. Disable this if you prefer to only import finalized transactions.

## Troubleshooting

### Connection Shows Error Status

If a connection enters an error state, the bank credentials may have expired or the provider encountered an issue. Click **Reconnect** to re-authenticate with your bank through the widget.

### Duplicate Transactions

Securo tracks each synced transaction by its external ID. If you see duplicates, it may be because you entered a transaction manually before it was synced. Securo attempts to match these automatically (within 3 days, same amount, 60%+ description similarity), but some edge cases may require manual cleanup.

### Missing Transactions

Some banks have delays in reporting transactions. If recent transactions are missing, try a manual sync after a few hours. Also check that **Import Pending** is enabled in your connection settings.

### Sync Not Running

Automatic sync requires Redis and Celery. Verify that:

- `REDIS_URL` is configured in your environment
- The Celery worker is running: `celery -A app.worker worker`
- The Celery beat scheduler is running: `celery -A app.worker beat`
